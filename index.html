Types of Television Technology

Each display technology works differently and suits different budgets, room sizes, and viewing habits.

🖤

OLED

Organic LED

Each pixel produces its own light and can switch off completely, delivering perfect blacks and infinite contrast. The gold standard for picture quality.

✔ Perfect blacks, infinite contrast ✔ Wide viewing angles ✔ Thin & lightweight panels ✘ Higher price point ✘ Risk of burn-in with static images
✨

QLED

Quantum LED

Samsung's quantum dot technology enhances LED backlighting to produce brighter, more vibrant colours. Excellent for bright, sunny rooms.

✔ Extremely bright — great for daylight ✔ Vivid, saturated colours ✔ No burn-in risk ✘ Blacks not as deep as OLED ✘ Narrower viewing angles
💡

LED / LCD

Standard LED

The most common TV type. Uses an LED backlight behind an LCD panel. Affordable, reliable, and available in all sizes from small to massive.

✔ Most affordable option ✔ Wide range of sizes ✔ Long lifespan ✘ Average contrast ratio ✘ Backlight bleeding possible
🔬

Mini-LED

Mini LED Backlit

Thousands of tiny LEDs behind the screen allow much more precise local dimming zones, closing the gap with OLED at a lower price point.

✔ Much better contrast than regular LED ✔ Very high brightness ✔ No burn-in risk ✘ More expensive than standard LED ✘ Some blooming around bright objects
🌈

QNED

Quantum NanoCell

LG's hybrid technology combining quantum dots with NanoCell filters and Mini-LED backlighting for accurate, wide colour gamut with strong brightness.

✔ Accurate, wide colour gamut ✔ Good viewing angles ✔ High brightness levels ✘ Complex tech at premium price ✘ Still behind OLED in contrast
🖥️

MicroLED

Next Generation

The future of TV. Microscopic self-emissive LEDs combine the best of OLED and QLED — perfect blacks, extreme brightness, no burn-in. Currently very expensive.

✔ Best of OLED + QLED combined ✔ Extremely long lifespan ✔ Modular — can change screen size ✘ Extremely expensive currently ✘ Limited consumer availability

How to Choose the Right TV

Buying a television is about matching the technology to your specific situation — your room brightness, viewing distance, content habits, and budget all matter.

An OLED looks breathtaking in a dark home theatre but can appear washed out in a sunlit living room where a QLED would thrive. A budget LED is perfectly fine for a bedroom but disappointing for a dedicated movie room.

  • 1 Check your room lighting. Bright rooms need high brightness (QLED/Mini-LED). Dark rooms benefit from OLED's perfect blacks.
  • 2 Consider your main use. Gaming needs 120Hz + low input lag. Movies need wide colour gamut. Sports need motion handling.
  • 3 Measure your viewing distance. For 4K to look sharp: sit 1.5× the screen size away. A 55" TV → sit ~7 feet back.
  • 4 Set a firm budget. OLED starts at ₹80,000. Excellent LED TVs exist from ₹25,000. Don't overspend on size you don't need.
  • 5 Check HDMI 2.1 ports. For next-gen consoles (PS5, Xbox), you need at least 2 × HDMI 2.1 ports for 4K@120Hz.
